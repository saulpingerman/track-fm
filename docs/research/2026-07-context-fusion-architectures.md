# Fusing time-varying spatial context into trajectory forecasters (verified review, 2026-07)

Verification key: [CONFIRMED] = 2/2 independent checks against primary source; [DISPUTED] = 1/2; [unaudited] = extracted but not adversarially checked. REFUTED claims appear only in §7.

---

## 1. Architecture patterns for fusing time-varying spatial fields into sequence models

Ranked by (a) verified evidence strength and (b) fit to TrackFM's egocentric continuous-density-grid output.

**1. Cross-attention + FiLM over per-timestep environmental encodings — the only pattern with a verified fusion-vs-fusion ablation in the AIS domain.** The closest published system to TrackFM (Hierarchical Two-Stage Env-Aware Vessel TP, 2026, https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16442) shows adaptive fusion (motion queries cross-attending to [spatial; env] keys/values + FiLM `(1+γ)h+β`) beats naive static concat of the *identical* environmental features: ADE 2.23 vs 2.51 km, no-env 2.65 km — **~67% of the total environmental gain comes from the fusion mechanism, not the data** [CONFIRMED]. Same paper: fields consumed as pointwise bilinear-spatial + linear-temporal interpolations to the vessel's exact position/AIS timestamp, and this alignment fidelity alone is worth an 11.2% ADE reduction vs coarse raw-resolution sampling [CONFIRMED]. Cross-attention is also the mechanism in the strongest known-future-covariate system (LT3P, 2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15726), chosen explicitly because it decouples gridded 3D field tokens from the 1D kinematic sequence [unaudited].

**2. Early fusion by token concatenation into one shared encoder — the best-verified pattern at scale (autonomous driving), directly compatible with a transformer trunk.** Wayformer (2022, https://arxiv.org/abs/2207.05844): per-modality linear projection → concat → single self-attention encoder is modality-agnostic and hit SOTA on WOMD and Argoverse, beating multi-encoder systems (minADE 0.545 vs MultiPath++ 0.556) [CONFIRMED]. Critical scale qualifier: "small models perform best with early fusion, but as model capacity increases, sensitivity to the choice of fusion decreases dramatically," and under ≤16 ms latency late fusion is optimal [CONFIRMED] — fusion choice matters most at TrackFM's likely (small-to-mid) scale. Forecast-MAE (2023, https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.09882) reached competitive SOTA at 1.9M params with pure token-concat early fusion, no cross-attention/raster/FiLM [unaudited]. Moirai (2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.02592) flattens every variate into one token sequence with Any-Variate Attention (RoPE + same/cross-variate binary biases) — covariates need no special module and any subset can be supplied or omitted [CONFIRMED] — but attention cost grows with variate count ("limited support for high-dimensional time series") [unaudited], so dense grids must be pooled first.

**3. Egocentric, heading-aligned, forward-shifted field crops → CNN → per-step feature — the proven aviation/geoscience pattern, the natural Stage-2 mechanism.** DeepTP (2018, https://arxiv.org/abs/1812.11670): 2°×2° (~120 nmi) 20×20×4 weather cube placed *in front of* the aircraft, rotated by course, CNN-compressed to 32-d, concatenated into the decoder each step [CONFIRMED]. Same crop-rotate-ahead design in the Bayesian aviation work (Pang et al., TR-C 2021, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trc.2021.103326): 32×32 EchoTop crop ahead of aircraft, rotated to heading, CNN-encoded, early-concatenated pre-LSTM [CONFIRMED] — though there the crop is squeezed to a single scalar/step, an unablated bottleneck [unaudited]. TC-track work (Giffard-Roisin et al., 2020, https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.10566) proved storm-centered crops decisive: fused 130 km vs kinematics-only 186.6 km at 24 h (30% cut); even wind-CNN alone (148.9 km) beat kinematics-only [CONFIRMED]. Its fusion recipe: pretrain each branch, concatenate last FC layers, **zero-initialize cross-branch weights** so the fused net starts as the ensemble of pretrained branches — worth 5 km vs joint from-scratch training [CONFIRMED].

**4. Early channel-concat of full field grids into a conv trunk — proven in ocean drift, where the field *is* the primary input.** DriftNet multivariate ablation (2026, https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03292): adding a field = adding channels to the first conv layer; no attention/FiLM/crops anywhere [CONFIRMED]. But: adding SST generally *hurts* (field misalignment/overfitting), useful field sets are regime-dependent, and idealized gains (>55% separation reduction) collapse to ~4% with real satellite/reanalysis inputs [all unaudited]. Rasterize-everything early fusion also reached Waymo SOTA (Occupancy Flow Fields, 2022, https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.03875) [unaudited].

**5. Graph/vector tokens + cross-attention for sparse structure (lanes), with context re-injected into the density decoder.** GOHOME (2022, https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.01827): lane graph via relation-specific graph convs, injected into agent features via Lanes2Agents cross-attention — no input rasterization [CONFIRMED]; matches dense-CNN accuracy (minFDE 1.26 vs 1.28) at 98% fewer FLOPs with sub-linear (vs quadratic) scaling in output range [CONFIRMED]. THOMAS (2022, https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.06607) re-injects lane features via cross-attention *at every level of the coarse-to-fine spatial decoder*, not just the encoder [CONFIRMED as mechanism]. VectorNet (2020, https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.04259): map context cut DE@3s by 14–25% over trajectory-only [CONFIRMED] — one of the few clean context-vs-no-context ablations anywhere in this corpus. Caveat: the sparse-token efficiency argument applies to natively sparse inputs (lanes, ports), not dense met-ocean grids (MultiPath++, 2021, https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.14973, made this argument explicit [CONFIRMED]; MultiPath++'s MCG gating also slightly beat transformer fusion at O(n+m) cost, minADE 0.978 vs 1.042 [CONFIRMED]).

**6. Gated injection of static/categorical attributes (don't early-concat them).** DualSTMA (2024, https://doi.org/10.3390/jmse12112031): early-concatenating vessel type *degraded* prediction (ADE 0.003616→0.003888) [CONFIRMED]; re-routing the same attributes through a multiplicative sigmoid gate before neighbor cross-attention flipped them to helpful (RMSE −3.5%, ADE −4.7%) [CONFIRMED]. TFT (2019, https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.09363) variable-selection networks + GLU gating give learned instance-wise channel suppression, worth +4.1% avg P90 when ablated [CONFIRMED] — but TFT's temporal backbone components dominate (>6–28% when ablated) vs context plumbing (1.9–4.1%) [unaudited]: build the kinematic trunk first.

**Anti-pattern evidence:** point-sampled/flattened field features with no spatial structure gave marginal or negative results — surface-current grids flattened into an MLP branch helped only moderately and mainly for near-stationary vessels (Slaughter et al., 2024, https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2468013324000081) [DISPUTED — 1/2 verifiers confirmed against full text; the other could not access it], and actively hurt one region/architecture (Region B 7.00→7.51 km) [CONFIRMED]; regional-mean buoy weather and nearest-neighbor vessels gave no benefit at all [unaudited].

## 2. Known-future covariates through the forecast horizon

**Feasibility and value are proven (typhoons):** LT3P (2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15726) conditions on NWP forecast fields spanning the full +6..+72 h horizon in real time, via cross-attention, beating operational ensembles (143.03 vs ECMWF-EPS 210.71 km 72 h FDE) [the specific "~78% vs best no-physics baseline" framing is refuted — see §7; the beat-ECMWF part checked out in verification].

**The reanalysis-vs-forecast quality mismatch is the dominant, verified failure mode:**
- LT3P: training on reanalysis but inferring on real-time forecast fields leaves a ~2× gap *even after* bias correction and fine-tuning (bias-corrected UM 143.03 km vs ERA5-oracle 70.94 km at 72 h) [CONFIRMED]. Pretraining on the long ERA5 archive is still necessary (UM-only training: 390.92 km) [unaudited]; a frozen ERA5-pretrained encoder alone underperforms until an explicit bias corrector maps forecasts into the pretraining distribution [unaudited].
- Load forecasting (Fay & Ringwood, 2010, https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/5405072/): the verified recipe is **train the predictive backbone on actual/analysis covariates, but calibrate the reliance/combination stage under forecast-quality covariates** — lowest error; setting reliance weights from clean-covariate statistics then deploying on forecasts was significantly worse [CONFIRMED]. And covariate forecast errors are serially correlated and structured (front-driven level shifts, cross-correlated across variables) — IID Gaussian noise augmentation is explicitly inadequate [CONFIRMED]. TrackFM's forecast-noise augmentation must use temporally and cross-channel correlated perturbations.

**Mechanisms for injecting horizon-time information:**
- **Masking-as-conditioning (best fit for TrackFM):** Scene Transformer (2021, https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.08417) pre-allocates future timesteps in the [agents × time] tensor; known-future values are injected by simply unmasking those slots — no architectural change [unaudited as mechanism, but the multi-task masking result is CONFIRMED, see §3]. Caveat: its only known-future *environmental* covariate (future traffic lights) was always masked, so the environmental case is architecturally supported but empirically untested there [unaudited].
- **Decoder-position routing:** TFT feeds known-future inputs to decoder/horizon positions with their own selection network, attended jointly with the encoded past — direct multi-horizon, no rollout [CONFIRMED].
- **Unmasked-token routing:** Moirai supplies horizon covariate values as unmasked tokens while only target-horizon tokens are masked, in a bidirectional encoder [CONFIRMED].
- **Adapter routing for a frozen backbone:** ChronosX (2025, https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.12107) injects past covariates at embeddings (IIB) and future-known covariates at output logits (OIB); frozen backbone + adapters improves WQL/MASE 22% over zero-shot [CONFIRMED]. But the claim that output-stage injection is *best* for future covariates is [DISPUTED] — never ablated against input-stage injection; and UniCA's ablation (see §7) found fusion position barely matters.
- **Recursive re-querying along predicted paths:** DeepTP re-matches forecast-product weather cubes around each newly predicted point at its predicted time [CONFIRMED] — but this commits to point trajectories (conflicting with direct multi-horizon density output) and its training was teacher-forced (exposure bias unaddressed) [CONFIRMED]. DriftNet (2023, https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.09818) shows the alternative: a non-Markovian one-shot design ingesting the full space-time field stack over the entire horizon avoids rollout error accumulation entirely [CONFIRMED]; its horizon fields are perfect-foresight reanalysis standing in for operational forecasts [CONFIRMED]. Likewise the DriftNet ablation paper feeds fields over the whole 9-day predicted window [CONFIRMED — with the caveat that these are reanalysis/hindcast products, not operational forecasts].

**Negative space:** the closest AIS analog (Hierarchical Two-Stage, 2026) achieves its 15.8% ADE gain from *past-only* env conditioning; forecast-interval injection is untouched [unaudited]. ClimaX (2023, https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.10343) never models a covariate *series* through the horizon (single-snapshot input + lead-time embedding), and cannot extrapolate beyond trained lead times; iterative rollout collapses past ~1 day [unaudited] — train over the full 0–4 h deployment horizon distribution.

## 3. Optional / missing conditioning — proven mechanisms

**Conditioning dropout with a learned null token (the strongest single result):** Classifier-Free Guidance (2022, https://arxiv.org/abs/2207.12598) — randomly replacing conditioning with a null token at rate p_uncond yields one network good both conditioned and unconditioned [CONFIRMED]. **Rates: 10–20% suffice; 50% is uniformly worse across the whole IS/FID frontier** (unguided FID 2.21 vs 1.80) [CONFIRMED]. Low, not uniform-heavy, per-channel masking rates.

**Whole-modality dropout (ModDrop, 2016, https://arxiv.org/abs/1501.00102):** dropping whole channels at 10% during fusion training gives a single network usable from any input subset at essentially zero clean-data cost (96.77→96.81%) [CONFIRMED]. Without it, jointly trained fusion silently over-relies on the dominant modality and collapses when it's absent (Jaccard 0.876→0.306 with mocap removed) [CONFIRMED]. Caveat for TrackFM: independent 10% drops almost never sample the all-channels-absent case (0.1^K) — the kinematics-only regime needs explicit structured subset sampling [unaudited].

**The dropout tax and its fix (AVSR, 2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.04245):** (a) a fusion model trained *without* modality dropout is worse than the unimodal baseline when the optional modality is missing (CER 26.65% vs audio-only 25.13%) [CONFIRMED] — hard dependence is the default failure. (b) Naive dropout training degrades best-case full-input accuracy, growing with rate (+0.58 CER @0.5, +2.12 @1.0) [CONFIRMED] — masking is not free. (c) Distilling a complete-data teacher's latent distribution into the dropout student (MDA-KD) recovers full-data accuracy *and* robustness simultaneously [unaudited]. Corroborating brittleness evidence in AIS: temporally shuffled env inputs cause "significant structural deviation" in the always-conditioned Hierarchical Two-Stage model [unaudited].

**Budgeted randomized masking across modalities (MultiMAE, 2022, https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.01678):** sampling per-modality visible-token budgets from Dirichlet(α=1) under a fixed total budget yields one encoder that runs on any modality subset with no retraining [CONFIRMED]; deterministic equal splits cost ~2 mIoU [unaudited].

**Missing-value representation — null embedding beats zero-fill:** Scene Transformer implements hidden/absent inputs by zeroing features *except a time embedding*, plus an explicit binary hidden-indicator bit; padding reuses the same pathway [CONFIRMED]. MoveFormer (2023, https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.03.05.531080v5) uses a *learnable* per-feature fill vector substituted for NaNs before projection [CONFIRMED]. CFG's learned null token [CONFIRMED] points the same way. Cheapest baseline: mean-impute + Boolean "was imputed" flag (Slaughter 2024) [unaudited]. Prefer learned absent-tokens + explicit indicator over silent zero-fill.

**What is NOT evidence of graceful degradation:** HOME (2021, https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.10968) drops raster channels at p=0.1 during training but *never evaluates inference with channels absent* — augmentation only [CONFIRMED]. ClimaX has no channel-dropout scheme and no withheld-variable evaluation; optionality is assumed from heterogeneous pretraining data [CONFIRMED] — though its per-variable tokenization + learnable-query cross-attention aggregation natively accepts arbitrary variable subsets [CONFIRMED], making it the right *architectural substrate*. Moirai varies variate *count* (beta-binomial sampling), not principled per-channel dropout [unaudited]. VectorNet's masked-node auxiliary loss improves the forecast itself (DE@3s 3.84→3.67) [CONFIRMED] but masking-in-training ≠ demonstrated robustness at inference [unaudited]. No paper in this corpus both trains with channel masking on gridded env fields *and* evaluates missing-at-inference degradation — TrackFM must build that evaluation itself.

## 4. Does context-conditioned pretraining help or hurt transfer?

**Help — when the context channel is itself masked:**
- Forecast-MAE (2023, https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.09882): jointly masking trajectory segments *and* map tokens in one MAE pretext, then fine-tuning, beats identical-architecture from-scratch (test minADE1 1.845→1.741, minADE6 0.727→0.709; extra scratch epochs don't close the gap) [CONFIRMED], and improves held-out-city OOD generalization [unaudited].
- MultiMAE: multimodal masked pretraining does **not** degrade single-modality transfer — RGB-only MultiMAE exactly matches RGB-only MAE downstream [CONFIRMED] — while the same weights convert extra fine-tune-time modalities into large gains a unimodal model can't exploit [unaudited]. So masked multimodal pretraining buys optionality at zero unimodal cost (but also no unimodal *gain*).
- Scene Transformer: one weight set trained with a mixture of masking patterns matches the unconditional-only model (minSADE 1.74 vs 1.76) — conditioning made optional at near-zero cost to the base task [CONFIRMED].

**Hurt — the verified failure modes:**
- **Shortcut learning:** in Forecast-MAE, leaving the (highly informative) future-trajectory channel *unmasked* during pretraining made pretraining actively harmful (minADE 0.864–0.866 vs 0.8314 scratch); masking it restored the gains [CONFIRMED]. Direct implication: forecast-interval environmental fields must be inside TrackFM's dropout scheme during pretraining, never always-present.
- **Cross-modal objectives can erode unimodal features:** vision+language pretraining produced no better transferable vision representations than vision-only supervised training across few-shot/linear-probe/clustering/robustness (Devillers et al., 2021, https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08313) [CONFIRMED]; the authors' mechanism — a pure cross-modal alignment objective doesn't constrain the encoder to stay domain-faithful, so it may discard features critical for downstream transfer [CONFIRMED].
- **Training dynamics, not capacity:** naively jointly-trained multimodal nets often lose to the best unimodal net (Kinetics RGB+Audio 71.4% vs RGB-only 72.6%; Wang et al., 2020, https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.12681) [CONFIRMED], because modalities overfit/generalize at different rates and one joint loss is suboptimal [CONFIRMED]; Gradient-Blending's per-modality loss reweighting fixes it (+2 to +5 pts) [unaudited].
- **Full fine-tuning can erode pretrained generalization:** UniCA (2025, https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.22039) — frozen backbone + covariate adapters beats fully finetuning the backbone with covariates (MAPE/CRPS) [CONFIRMED]. ClimaX similarly reports frozen-attention finetuning beating full finetuning in low-data regimes [unaudited].
- **Frozen backbones impose their own dynamics:** Aurora frozen-latent decoders make soil moisture fluctuate at atmospheric timescales (Lehmann et al., 2025, https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.19088) [unaudited]; new variables decode well only when physically correlated with pretraining variables [note: the strong version of this claim was refuted on one sub-point — §7]. ClimaX transfers poorly to field families absent from pretraining (precipitation) [unaudited] — expose the field encoder to every field family you'll condition on.

**The kinematics-only counterfactual:** UniTraj (2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.03859) shows a lat/lon/time-only pretrained model transferring across recovery/prediction/classification/generation — but its zero-shot superiority is [DISPUTED] (it loses to baselines on 3/6 recovery datasets zero-shot; only fine-tuned wins everywhere), and its context integration is explicitly future work [unaudited]. **No paper anywhere in this corpus runs the experiment TrackFM actually needs: context-conditioned vs kinematics-only pretraining compared on downstream transfer.** That ablation is a publishable contribution, not a literature lookup.

## 5. Closest analogs to TrackFM's grid-density output, and decoder lessons

- **AISFormer (2025, https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0029801825017822)** — the nearest neighbor: four-hot discretized position/velocity, multi-head grid classification, multi-resolution CE, ERA5 wave fusion; sub-10-nmi error to 9–10 h on North Atlantic lanes [CONFIRMED, abstract-level only]. Its >20% gain is vs TrAISformer, **not** a no-wave ablation of itself — it is *not* clean evidence that wave conditioning helps a grid-CE forecaster [CONFIRMED]. Full text paywalled, no preprint; the wave-fusion mechanism and any wave ablation table are worth pulling via PSU library [unaudited pitfall note].
- **HOME (2021, https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.10968)** — endpoint heatmap + Gaussian soft targets + focal loss; the verified lesson is **post-hoc tunable samplers**: greedy max-coverage (MR-optimal) and k-means refinement (FDE-optimal) let each consumer pick its own operating point from one trained density without retraining, with a verified MR/FDE conflict per extraction step [CONFIRMED]. Heatmap-vs-regression ablation (MR6 6.8% vs 13.0%) [unaudited].
- **GOHOME / THOMAS** — density heads need not be dense: lane-anchored Frenet rasters projected to Cartesian match dense decoding at ~2% of FLOPs [CONFIRMED]; hierarchical coarse-to-fine sparse decoding evaluates 1,856 of 147,456 cells with no performance loss [CONFIRMED]. Lesson for a 4 h maritime grid (severe resolution-vs-coverage trade-off, HOME's 72 m/3 s grid never faced it [unaudited]): coarse-to-fine sparse evaluation and lane-anchored components are proven levers; but lane-restricted mass slightly cedes off-graph coverage (GOHOME test MR 10.5 vs dense HOME 9.8) [unaudited] — vessels leave lanes, keep an off-lane component.
- **THOMAS recombination** — scene-consistent multi-agent futures can be added *on top of* per-agent marginal density grids via a learned endpoint-recombination head, backbone unchanged [CONFIRMED] — the right template for TrackFM's later multi-vessel stage (demonstrated on road agents, not vessels).
- **ImplicitO (2023, https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.01471)** — continuous query-point decoding: with identical encoder/training, an implicit (x,y,t)-query decoder beats an explicit grid decoder, dramatically in the fast regime (HwySim mAP 0.648→0.716) [CONFIRMED], and the gain comes almost entirely from letting each query predict *where to look* (K=1 deformable offset + cross-attention, 0.649→0.716) [CONFIRMED] — a far-future query needs features from where the evidence was. Fits TrackFM's Fourier-continuous density naturally; extend continuity to the time axis [unaudited supporting result: interpolating explicit grids to off-grid times systematically misses moving occupancy].
- **Occupancy Flow Fields (2022, https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.03875)** — single-pass multi-horizon dense decoding at scale [unaudited]; backward-flow auxiliary fields capture multimodal futures with one vector per cell, beating K=3 forward-flow fields [CONFIRMED]; a flow-trace consistency loss buys identity/motion coherence (ID recall 89.9→95.0%) at the cost of early-timestep flow accuracy [CONFIRMED].
- **StopNet (2022, https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.00991)** — co-training an occupancy-grid head with a trajectory head on one encoder improved trajectory metrics on all three datasets, never hurting [CONFIRMED], coupled only by a cheap consistency loss (CE between predicted grid and grid rendered from own predicted trajectories) [CONFIRMED]. Evidence is one-directional (grid→trajectory measured; trajectory→grid unreported) [unaudited]. Multi-head grid+point training is safe and probably helpful for TrackFM's downstream heads.
- **Y-Net (2021, https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.01526)** — same BCE-vs-Gaussian-soft-target grid loss; verified lessons: factorizing uncertainty into multimodal goal density + goal-conditioned path density is what makes 6× longer horizons tractable (advantage over PECNet roughly doubles 4.8 s→30 s) [CONFIRMED], and conditioning on intermediate *waypoints* matters for path accuracy specifically (removing CWS: 30 s ADE +24.3%, FDE unchanged) [CONFIRMED]. For TrackFM: consider hierarchical horizon conditioning (4 h goal density conditioning intermediate-horizon densities) rather than fully independent per-horizon heads.
- **deepSSF (2026, https://besjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/2041-210X.70136)** — grid-CE next-step density factorized as habitat-selection × movement-kernel surfaces added in log space then normalized [CONFIRMED]; sin/cos time covariates broadcast to constant spatial channels and recomputed each rollout step [CONFIRMED] — the recompute-per-step pattern is exactly how forecast-time fields would feed each horizon. Its cautionary result: curated covariates overfit out-of-sample while raw spectral bands generalized [unaudited].

Evaluation lessons: overlap metrics reward overconfidence on static-heavy data (64% of AV2 actors static — directly analogous to anchored vessels); stratify moving vs stationary and report calibration (ECE) alongside density metrics (ImplicitO) [unaudited]. Grid heads ensemble by simple probability averaging (>15% MR gain, GOHOME) [unaudited].

## 6. Concrete recommendations mapped to TrackFM's staged plan

**Stage 1 — local (along-track) environmental features on kinematic tokens:**
1. Interpolate fields bilinearly in space and linearly in time to each AIS ping — alignment fidelity alone is worth ~11% (2605.16442 [CONFIRMED]). Do not join to nearest coarse snapshot.
2. **CHANGE to plan:** do not naive-concat even at Stage 1. The verified AIS ablation puts ~2/3 of the env gain in the fusion mechanism (cross-attn+FiLM vs concat, 2605.16442 [CONFIRMED]), and static/categorical channels concat-fused actively hurt until gated (DualSTMA [CONFIRMED]). Minimum viable Stage 1: per-channel projection + GLU/sigmoid gating (TFT-style VSN is the proven cheap version [CONFIRMED]). This pulls "adaptive fusion" forward from Stage 3.
3. Represent missing values with learned null embeddings + explicit presence bits (Scene Transformer [CONFIRMED], MoveFormer [CONFIRMED], CFG [CONFIRMED]), not zero-fill.
4. Run the context-vs-no-context ablation from day one — almost no prior work has one (Wayformer, Scene Transformer, HOME, OFF, ImplicitO, GOHOME, StopNet, DriftNet all lack it), and expect the aggregate gain to be diluted: ~92% of an AIS benchmark was open-water/weak-interaction [CONFIRMED, EnvShip-Bench 2026, https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15240]; currents helped mainly near-stationary vessels (Slaughter [DISPUTED]); most aviation deviations had no weather pattern (Pang [unaudited]). Report gains stratified by weather-exposure/regime subsets, not just averages.

**Stage 2 — egocentric field crop into the density head:**
5. Crop **ahead of and rotated to** the vessel, sized to the reachable set at the forecast horizon, not centered on current position: verified in DeepTP [CONFIRMED] and Pang [CONFIRMED]; VectorNet's raster baseline needed trajectory-covering crops to be competitive [unaudited]. At 4 h × ~20 kn this dictates a large, coarse, forward-biased crop.
6. **CHANGE:** condition the *decoder*, not only the trunk — THOMAS re-injects context at every refinement level of the spatial decoder [CONFIRMED as mechanism], and ImplicitO's query-conditioned deformable readout (each density query predicts where in the field to look) is the single verified largest driver of its gains [CONFIRMED]. For a Fourier-continuous head, ImplicitO's continuous (x,y,t) query decoding is the best-matched precedent; keep K small (K=4 ≤ K=1) and monitor calibration [unaudited].
7. Don't over-compress: Pang's crop→scalar bottleneck is a cautionary unablated design [unaudited]; keep ≥32-d crop embeddings (DeepTP [CONFIRMED]).
8. Preserve within-crop location if using conv encoders (DeepTP deliberately omitted pooling/padding) [unaudited].

**Stage 3 — full spatiotemporal cross-attention over field tokens (incl. forecast-interval fields):**
9. Known-future fields: allocate horizon-position tokens and feed forecast fields as unmasked tokens there (Scene Transformer mechanism [unaudited]; TFT decoder routing [CONFIRMED]; Moirai unmasked horizon covariates [CONFIRMED]). One-shot full-horizon ingestion avoids rollout error accumulation (DriftNet [CONFIRMED]); do not plan recursive re-querying (DeepTP's variance-explosion apparatus [unaudited], point-commitment conflict [CONFIRMED]).
10. **CHANGE (biggest one):** the plan must include a forecast-product adaptation path, not reanalysis-only training. LT3P's ~2× residual gap after bias correction [CONFIRMED] and Fay & Ringwood's two-stage split (backbone on analysis fields; reliance/gating calibrated under forecast-quality fields, with *correlated* synthetic forecast errors) [CONFIRMED] jointly say: train the field encoder on the long reanalysis archive, then adapt the conditioning pathway on paired forecast/analysis data (GFS/HRES vs ERA5) before deployment.
11. **CHANGE:** forecast-interval field channels go inside the masking curriculum during pretraining — an always-present informative future channel poisons the pretext via shortcut learning (Forecast-MAE, 0.864 vs 0.8314 [CONFIRMED]).
12. Masking curriculum: per-channel drop rates ~10–20%, not 50% (CFG [CONFIRMED]); whole-channel drops à la ModDrop [CONFIRMED]; plus explicit structured subsets including the all-context-dropped kinematics-only case (independent low-rate drops never sample it [unaudited]); Dirichlet-style randomized budgets beat fixed splits (MultiMAE [CONFIRMED / ratio ablation unaudited]). Expect a small full-input tax; if it matters, add complete-data teacher distillation (MDA-KD [unaudited]).
13. Field tokenization for many channels: ClimaX's per-variable tokens + learnable-query cross-attention aggregation is the verified substrate for arbitrary channel subsets at fixed sequence length [CONFIRMED]; compress with latent queries before any global attention (Wayformer 2–16× speedup [unaudited]; VectorNet's efficiency argument collapses for dense grids [unaudited]).
14. Foundation-model hygiene: keep downstream adaptation frozen-backbone-first (UniCA [CONFIRMED], ClimaX low-data [unaudited]); pretrain the field encoder on every field family you'll condition on (ClimaX precipitation failure [unaudited]); consider adding a kinematics-only self-supervised loss term alongside any cross-modal objective to keep representations domain-faithful (Devillers mechanism [CONFIRMED], proposed fix [unaudited]).
15. Add a cheap co-trained point/occupancy consistency head (StopNet [CONFIRMED]) and interpretability probes on context usage (TFT attention diagnostics [unaudited]) to audit over-/under-reliance per channel.

## 7. Refuted / disputed claims and honest gaps

**REFUTED (do not cite these framings):**
- *LT3P cuts 72 h error ~78% vs the best no-physics data-driven baseline.* MGTCF uses ERA5 (not no-physics), PECNet (518.46 km) was the better no-physics baseline (→ ~72%), MGTCF was author-modified and evaluated on 2019 only. The real-time full-horizon forecast-field conditioning and the beat-ECMWF result stand. (LT3P, 2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15726)
- *Pang et al. sample known-future weather along the filed flight plan.* Code shows cubes are matched around the **actual flown track** — a ground-truth-future-path oracle, strictly stronger leakage than a plan prior. Any TrackFM analog (lane prior / dead-reckoning path) substitutes for the *true* future path, a larger gap than claimed. (TR-C 2021, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trc.2021.103326)
- *UniCA shows known-future covariates should be injected post-encoder.* UniCA's own ablation: fusion position has "relatively minor impact"; pre-encoder fusion of future covariates works essentially as well; concat-into-input was never tested. (2025, https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.22039)
- *Hodgkin et al.'s 20–22.5% skill gain came from weather-scalar fusion.* The gain is from the full 14-feature vector including operator/route/altitude features, which dominate importance; no met-only ablation exists. Also *"cross-track wind was empirically uninformative"* — it was excluded a priori, and the informative comparator (wind_magnitude) is frame-independent, undercutting the "egocentric decomposition carries the signal" thesis. (2026, https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03152)
- *EnvShip-Bench shows concat-fused static geography fails to beat trajectory-only baselines.* The numbers are real and env models indeed don't beat the best trajectory-only baselines, but the paper never specifies concatenation as the fusion mechanism, and 92% open-water samples dilute any gain — it cannot support an anti-early-concat design argument. (2026, https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15240)
- *Aurora frozen-latent failures (incl. terrestrial water storage) are recovered by full fine-tuning.* TWS was never fine-tuned; recovery is shown only for snowmelt runoff and soil-moisture dynamics. The correlation-governs-frozen-transfer direction survives in weakened form. (Lehmann et al., 2025, https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.19088)

**DISPUTED (one verifier objected; use with the stated caveat):**
- ChronosX OIB-for-future-covariates as "best" injection point — mechanism confirmed, optimality never ablated (https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.12107).
- Aurora decoder efficiency: "~80× fewer FLOPS" is an order-of-magnitude misread (Table 2: ~8×); the 50% time / 35% memory / PCC 0.95+ figures verified (https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.19088).
- UniTraj universal zero-shot transfer — loses zero-shot on 3/6 recovery datasets and its headline number is on its own pretraining corpus (https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.03859); its four-masking-strategy mechanism story is also partly contradicted by its own ablations (removing block masking *improves* 4/6 datasets).
- Slaughter et al. currents-mainly-help-drifting-vessels — confirmed verbatim by one verifier against the OA full text; the other could not access the paper (https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2468013324000081).

**Honest gaps in the literature (TrackFM must generate this evidence itself):**
1. **No context-vs-no-context ablation** exists in most SOTA context-conditioned forecasters (Wayformer, Scene Transformer, OFF, ImplicitO, HOME, GOHOME, THOMAS, StopNet, MultiPath++, Y-Net, DriftNet, Pang). The clean quantifications that do exist: VectorNet (14–25% [CONFIRMED]), Giffard-Roisin (30% [CONFIRMED]), Hierarchical Two-Stage (15.8% ADE [ablation table CONFIRMED]), and they span domains where fields are strongly vs weakly coupled to motion.

## Completeness critic (what this review may have missed)

(a1) Vessel–vessel interaction as time-varying context (traffic density fields, neighbor encoders, TrAISformer-line social variants) has no lens — DualSTMA's neighbor cross-attention enters only incidentally, yet multi-vessel is TrackFM's stated later stage.

(a2) Physics/hybrid baselines are absent: leeway/OpenDrift-style drift models, ship hydrodynamic response to wind/wave, and residual-learning or physics-informed losses against them — the natural competitor to learned field fusion in the maritime domain.

(a3) Irregular/asynchronous sampling on the sequence side (Neural CDEs, continuous-time attention, mTAN-style imputation-aware TS models) is missed — field-to-ping alignment is covered, but AIS ping irregularity interacting with per-timestep env tokens is not.

(a4) Conditioned generative trajectory forecasters (diffusion/flow: MotionDiffuser, LED, MID) are absent — CFG is imported only from image synthesis, so conditioning-dropout evidence in-domain for density-output forecasters was never searched.

(a5) Probabilistic covariates: no coverage of conditioning on NWP *ensembles* (or forecast-uncertainty channels) rather than a single deterministic field — directly relevant since §2's dominant failure mode is forecast-field error.

(a6) Output-space constraints (land masking, coastline-aware density normalization, reachability priors) for an ocean-scale grid head — no lens touches keeping probability mass off land, a problem driving heatmaps at ≤192 m never face.
